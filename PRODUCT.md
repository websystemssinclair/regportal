# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed community college students — both returning adults (balancing work, family, and scheduling constraints) and traditional-age students (mobile-first, low tolerance for friction). They arrive task-focused: find a section, build a schedule, register. They're not here to browse; they're here to act before seats fill.

## Product Purpose

RegPortal is Sinclair College's course registration portal. Students search for Sections, plan a conflict-free Schedule with the Schedule Builder, and register — all in one place. The product succeeds when a student can go from "I need to register for fall" to "done" without confusion, friction, or error. Every screen serves one of those steps.

## Brand Personality

Comfortable, efficient, modern.

The interface should feel like a well-designed tool the student already knows how to use — not a government form, not a sales page. Calm like Notion. Fast and direct. The school brand (crimson) is present but quiet; it earns trust through consistency, not decoration.

## References

- **Notion** — calm, document-first, content gets the space. The brand accent is a single accent; the rest is neutral and readable. That restraint is the model.

## Anti-references

Generic, default, out-of-the-box college portals (Blackboard, Banner Self-Service, legacy SIS UIs). Anything that feels like it was configured, not designed. Banner-heavy, overcrowded, or visually inconsistent.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool should disappear into the task.** Students are not here to admire the interface. Every visual decision should reduce friction, not add personality for its own sake.
2. **Calm is the brand.** A registration portal is a high-stress moment — seats are filling, schedules are complicated. Calm, readable UI reduces that anxiety. Restraint over richness.
3. **State is always visible.** Loading, error, empty, waitlisted, closed — every section status and system state must be legible at a glance. Ambiguity costs students seats.
4. **Consistency earns trust.** The same button shape, the same form controls, the same icon vocabulary screen to screen. Consistency signals quality. Surprise signals unreliability.
5. **Accessible is not optional.** Mixed audience includes older returning students and users on assistive tech. WCAG AA is the floor, not a stretch goal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Keyboard-navigable throughout. Sufficient contrast on all text (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text). Reduced-motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color is never the sole signal for state (always paired with text or icon). Consider reduced vision when choosing tap target sizes (minimum 44×44px on mobile).
